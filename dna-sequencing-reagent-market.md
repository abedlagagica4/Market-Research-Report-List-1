<p><p>The DNA sequencing reagent market comprises three primary types: Sanger sequencing, Next Generation Sequencing (NGS), and Third Generation Sequencing (TGS). Sanger sequencing, the traditional method, relies on capillary electrophoresis for determining DNA sequences with high accuracy but lower throughput. NGS revolutionizes the field by enabling rapid sequencing of millions of fragments simultaneously, making it ideal for large-scale genomic studies. TGS further pushes the boundaries, allowing for real-time, long-read sequencing, which enhances the understanding of complex genomes and structural variations.</p></p>
