<p><p>The geotechnical sensors market encompasses various instruments used to monitor ground conditions. Extensometers measure changes in length to assess soil or rock movement. Piezometers gauge pore water pressure in soil, providing insights on water flow and stability. Rain gauges collect precipitation data, crucial for understanding hydrological impacts on geotechnical conditions. Thermometers monitor temperature variations, affecting material behavior. Additionally, "Others" includes diverse sensors like tiltmeters and strain gauges, enhancing site-specific data collection for geotechnical engineering applications.</p></p>
