<p><p>In the microscope digital cameras market, two primary sensor types dominate: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device). CMOS sensors are valued for their fast processing speeds, lower power consumption, and ability to integrate additional functions on the chip, making them ideal for real-time imaging. In contrast, CCD sensors are known for their superior image quality and sensitivity, providing better performance in low-light conditions. Each type serves distinct applications in scientific, medical, and educational fields, influencing purchasing decisions.</p></p>
