<p><p>The RF semiconductors market comprises three primary types: Gallium Arsenide (GaAs), Gallium Nitride (GaN), and Silicon (Si). GaAs is celebrated for its superior electron mobility and high-frequency performance, making it ideal for applications like satellite communications and smartphones. GaN offers high efficiency and power density, suitable for high-frequency and high-power applications, such as radar systems and wireless infrastructure. Silicon, though less efficient at high frequencies, remains popular for cost-effective, lower-power applications in consumer devices and traditional communication systems.</p></p>
