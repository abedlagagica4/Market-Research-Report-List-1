<p><p>The millifluidic chips market is categorized into 2.5D and 3D designs. The 2.5D chips feature layers that are stacked vertically but retain a primarily planar layout, facilitating complex fluidic paths while remaining easier to manufacture. In contrast, 3D millifluidic chips introduce a fully three-dimensional structure, allowing for intricate channel networks and enhanced fluid interactions. This design enables more sophisticated processes, such as enhanced mixing and separation, making them suitable for diverse applications in biomedical research and chemical analysis.</p></p>
