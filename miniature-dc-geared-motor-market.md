<p><p>The miniature DC geared motor market is segmented into two main types: brushed and brushless motors. Brushed DC motors use brushes for commutation, providing simplicity and cost-effectiveness, making them popular in low-cost applications. They tend to have a shorter lifespan due to wear on the brushes. Conversely, brushless DC motors eliminate brushes, allowing for higher efficiency, longer life, and reduced maintenance. They are ideal for applications requiring precise control and increased durability, such as robotics and automation.</p></p>
